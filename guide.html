Dolphin Stadium

Always open, even outside performance times.
If you approach the side of the pool, the dolphins
might even come up to you.

Penguin

You can see what’s happening in the water from the
1st floor, and what’s happening on the hill from the
2nd floor. The gap between their toddling walk on
land and the dynamic way they plunge through the
water is irresistible.

Giant salamander

The giant salamander is a Special Natural
Monument of Japan. Described as a living
fossil and believed to be one of the world’s
largest amphibians, there are many mysteries
surrounding the ecology of this extremely rare
animal. In an effort to let more people know
about the appeal of the giant salamander,
because its body looks like the number 9, and
because it becomes more active in breeding
season around September, the ninth month of
the year, Kyoto Aquarium applied to the Japan
Anniversary Association for September 9th to
be declared “Salamander Day,” and that
application was approved.

the Sea of Hong Kong

The Sea of HK is a huge pool holding about
500 tons of artificial seawater that recreates
the Kyoto Sea, which is brimming with many
different varieties of aquatic life. There are
seven viewing spots, including overhead
windows and caves.
